Client / Developer Project Report

Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

I chose content based on my interest in photography and what I would want on a real photography website. I used photos that I have taken myself for the portfolio and looked at other photography websites for ideas on the layout and organization.

How did you communicate with your developer? Were there any issues with communication?

My developer and I communicated through Microsoft Teams. We shared my site plan, photos, and feedback. Communication went well overall, although sometimes it took a little while for everyone to respond.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

Yes. The website matched my site plan well. I only requested one change, which was replacing the About Me photo with a different picture.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

Next time I would communicate more often and check in throughout the project instead of waiting until the end to review the website.

Lead Developer Report

How did you manage your development team and communicate the tasks for your junior developers?

I shared the site plan with my junior developers and made sure they knew which pages they were responsible for. We communicated through Microsoft Teams and GitHub to coordinate our work.

How did you communicate with your client? Were there any issues?

I communicated with my client through Microsoft Teams. I shared updates, sent the published website link, and asked for feedback. Communication went smoothly overall.

How did you handle publishing your client's site and sharing it with the client?

I published the website using GitHub Pages. After checking that everything worked correctly, I sent the website link to my client and asked if they wanted any changes.

How was your experience managing your site with GitHub?

GitHub was confusing at first, especially learning how to pull, commit, push, and avoid overwriting other people's work. After practicing, I became much more comfortable using it.

Junior Developer Report

How did you communicate with your team lead? Were there any issues?

I communicated with both team leads through Microsoft Teams. There were a few delays while waiting for repositories and website links, but once everything was shared, communication went well.

Did you know what was expected of you for each site?

Yes. I was assigned the Housing page for one project and the Fitness Tips page for another. My team leads explained which pages I needed to create and update.

Were you able to contribute to your sites?

Yes. I completed the Housing page by improving the content and adding another section about Utah County housing. I also created the Fitness Tips page using the client's site plan and the shared stylesheet.

Did you run into any issues with GitHub and adding your changes to the sites?

Yes. At first I had trouble learning how to pull, push, and work with repositories without overwriting other people's changes. After getting more experience, I understood the workflow much better.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

The most challenging part was learning GitHub and working with multiple repositories at the same time. It took some practice, but by the end of the project I felt much more comfortable using GitHub.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

I enjoyed working on real websites and seeing everyone's projects come together. It was interesting to experience being both a client and a developer.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

I would communicate with my teammates earlier and check the repositories more often so everyone stayed on the same page throughout the project.

Do you feel more confident working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

Yes. This project gave me experience using GitHub, working with a team, communicating with clients, and making changes to shared projects. I feel much more confident working on collaborative web development projects than I did before.
